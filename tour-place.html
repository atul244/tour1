Jaisalmer Tour

Day 1

Jaisalmer Fort

This is one of the "living fort" as almost one-fourth population of the whole city has been living within the fort. The whole fort construction ( including havelis, shops, temple everything ) is made up of yellow sandstone which gives the appearance of SONAR KELLA or GOLDEN FORT. Fort area is huge and you may find yourself lost somewhere, but people are very friendly, they always will help you to get the right direction.

Day 2

Longewala War Memorial

Longewala war memorial is 2 hours drive from the Jaisalmer, The Battle of Longewala (4–7 December 1971) was one of the first major engagements in the western sector during the Indo-Pakistani War of 1971.

Day 3

Jain Temple

Inside the Jaisalmer fort, This amazing architecture temple, with really beautiful designs carved on stone walls. is a place you might want to visit in Rajasthan

Day 4

Kothari's Patwaon-Ki-Haveli

Anywhere you go in the city of Jaisalmer, you are sure to set sights on the delicate carvings of the various ‘havelis’, and Patwon ki Haveli is one such monument. Situated in the vicinity of the Jaisalmer fort, you can take a vehicle to a certain point then go by foot, or if you fancy marveling at the beautiful sand-stone structures, walk the entire distance.This ‘haveli’ is the most important and the biggest in Jaisalmer.

Day 5

Tanot Mata Temple

Situated 120km North West of Jaisalmer city is Tanot in Thar Desert of Rajasthan. You will find sand dumes all along with small bushes. You can see herds of sheep & goats on the way. Beautiful landscape. Total desert. Nice to see Indira Gandhi Canal full of water after Ramgarh. You can see vegetation & farms near the canal. It is very near to Pakistan border. Last civilian point in India on border.

Day 6

Kuldhara Abandoned Village

Kuldhara is one of the 84 neighboring village which were inhabited by the Paliwal Brahmins tribe. There are many reasons given for abandoning the villages, some says its because of the shortage of water or an earthquake made everyone to abandon village. As per local villagers, one of the minister of the Jaisalmer king was attracted to the beautiful girl of the this village and he asked them to hand over her to him.
